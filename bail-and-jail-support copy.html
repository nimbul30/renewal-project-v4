Bail & Jail Support

A practical guide to setting up and running a bail fund and,
providing jail support for activists.

Why Jail Support is Crucial?

When people are arrested during protests, the state aims to demoralize them and disrupt the movement. Jail support is a powerful act of solidarity. It means ensuring that no one feels alone from the moment of arrest to their release. A friendly face, a snack, and a ride home can make a world of difference.


Setting Up a Bail Fund

A visual roadmap of the key steps to create a community-focused bail fund.

1. Form a Core Team

Gather a small, dedicated group of volunteers. You'll need people to manage finances, answer the support hotline, and coordinate logistics.

2. Partner with Legal Experts

Connect with local lawyers, legal aid societies, or chapters of the National Lawyers Guild. Their expertise is invaluable.

3. Fundraise

Start a fundraising campaign. Be transparent about how the money will be used. Crowdfunding platforms and community events can be effective.

4. Establish a Hotline

Set up a dedicated phone number that arrested individuals or their contacts can call. Ensure this number is widely distributed.

5. Understand the Local System

Learn the ins and outs of your local jails and courts. Where are people taken? How is bail posted? What payment methods are accepted?
