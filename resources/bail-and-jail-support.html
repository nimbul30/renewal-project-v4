Bail & Jail Support

A practical guide to setting up and running a bail fund and,
providing jail support for activists.

Why Jail Support is Crucial?

When people are arrested during protests, the state aims to demoralize them and disrupt the movement. Jail support is a powerful act of solidarity. It means ensuring that no one feels alone from the moment of arrest to their release. A friendly face, a snack, and a ride home can make a world of difference.


Setting Up a Bail Fund: Key Steps

A bail fund is a revolving pool of money used to pay bail for community members. Here’s a basic roadmap to setting one up:


  1. Form a Core Team: Gather a small, dedicated group of volunteers. You'll need people to manage finances, answer the support hotline, and coordinate logistics.

  2. Form a Core Team: Gather a small, dedicated group of volunteers. You'll need people to manage finances, answer the support hotline, and coordinate logistics.

  3. Partner with Legal Experts: Connect with local lawyers, legal aid societies, or chapters of the National Lawyers Guild. Their expertise is invaluable.

  4. Fundraise: Start a fundraising campaign. Be transparent about how the money will be used. Crowdfunding platforms can be effective, as can community events.

  5. Establish a Hotline: Set up a dedicated phone number that arrested individuals or their contacts can call. Make sure this number is widely distributed before any action (written on arms in permanent marker, for example).

  6. Understand the Local System: Learn the ins and outs of your local jails and courts. Where are people taken after arrest? How is bail posted? What payment methods are accepted? This information is critical.

  • Be Prepared to Wait:
    The release process can take hours. Bring water, snacks, portable chargers, and warm clothes for yourselves and for those being released.

  • Track Arrests: Have a system for tracking who has been arrested. Get their full legal name and date of birth. This is essential for locating them in the system.

  • Coordinate Transportation: Arrange for volunteer drivers to give released individuals a safe ride home or to a safe location.

  • Provide Post-Release Support: Offer food, water, and a chance to decompress. Provide information about upcoming court dates and connect them with legal resources. Document any injuries or instances of police misconduct.
