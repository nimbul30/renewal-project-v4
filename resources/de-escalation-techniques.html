Keepin' It Chill

A Real-Talk Guide to De-Escalation at Protests



So, Why Bother with De-Escalation?


Image by Noe Nei from Pixabay


Look, things can get heated out there. Knowing how to chill a situation out is a superpower. It keeps you and your people safe, makes sure your message actually gets heard instead of being drowned out by chaos, and it's the core of peaceful protest. This guide has your back with some real-world tips.

The Basic Vibe

1. Keep Your Cool

Your vibe affects the whole scene. If you're calm, it helps others be calm too. Take a deep breath and remember why you're there. You got this.

2. Just Listen

Sometimes, angry people just want to feel heard. Let them talk. You don't have to agree, but just showing you're listening can bring the tension way down.

3. Be Respectful

Talk in a normal, calm voice. No need for insults or yelling. A little respect goes a long way, even with people you totally disagree with.

How to Handle It: Words & Actions

What to Say

  • Use "I" statements: Instead of "You're being aggressive," try "I'm starting to feel unsafe with the shouting." It's less accusatory.
  • Give them a choice: "Hey, can we take a step apart so it's easier to hear each other?" This gives them a little control.
  • Acknowledge their feelings: "I hear that you're frustrated, but..." It shows you're listening without agreeing with bad behavior.

Body Language Hacks

  • Look open: Keep your hands where people can see them. Don't cross your arms—it looks defensive.
  • Give 'em space: Don't crowd people. Stand at a bit of an angle so nobody feels trapped.
  • Check your face: Try to look neutral or concerned. Smiling can come off as sarcastic, and scowling is just asking for a fight.

Know Your Rights (The Short Version)

Knowing your rights is key. This isn't legal advice, but here's the gist from the ACLU: Great info and support at: aclu.com!


  • You can protest on public sidewalks, streets, and in parks.

  • You can record anything you see in public, and that includes the police.

  • Cops can't shut a protest down unless things are getting dangerous.

  • If a cop stops you, ask "Am I free to go?" If they arrest you, stay quiet and ask for a lawyer.

    Protest Prep & First Aid 101

    Your Pre-Protest Checklist:

    • Have a buddy. Don't go alone.
    • Tell someone your plan.
    • Write your emergency contact number on your arm.
    • Phone fully charged? Good. Got a backup battery? Even better.
    • Comfy shoes are a must.

    Your Protest Go-Bag:

    • Water and a granola bar or something.
    • A few bandaids and wet wipes.
    • Water mixed with a little liquid antacid (like Maalox) in a squirt bottle helps with tear gas.
    • ID and a bit of cash.

    Quick First Aid:

    • Tear Gas/Pepper Spray: Don't rub your face! Blink a lot and flush your eyes with your water/antacid mix.
    • Scrapes: Press on it with something clean, then wipe it down.
    • Feeling Woozy from Heat: Find shade, sip water, and put a wet cloth on your neck.

    Pass this around. Stay safe out there.
